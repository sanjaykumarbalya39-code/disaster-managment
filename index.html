Frameworks & Standards

ICS / NIMS — Incident Command Structure
Our platforms integrate with ICS organizational structures and NIMS resource typing — making our tools compatible with any Federal or State coordinated response.
Sphere Standards — Humanitarian Minimums
Relief supply kits and medical deployments are calibrated to Sphere Project thresholds for shelter, water, food, and health across displaced population scenarios.
ISO 22320 — Emergency Management
Interoperability, command structures, and information management aligned to the international standard for incident response.
WHO HEICS — Hospital Emergency Standards
Medical equipment and field hospital configurations meeting Hospital Emergency Incident Command System requirements for surge capacity.
SENDAI Framework — Risk Reduction
Pre-positioning and preparedness programs aligned to the UN's global disaster risk reduction priorities through 2030.
CBRN — Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear
Specialized equipment kits for CBRN incident management, contamination monitoring, mass decontamination, and protective protocols.

FAQ

Operational Questions Answered

Common questions from procurement offices, field commanders, and emergency coordinators.

How quickly can equipment be deployed to a disaster zone?
We maintain pre-positioned stockpiles at 14 global depot locations. Standard deployment is 72 hours to most regions; premium accounts with pre-staged kits can achieve same-day or next-day field delivery via air freight partnerships.
Can your systems integrate with existing government EOC platforms?
Yes. Our command integration layer supports API connectivity with common EOC platforms, WebEOC, Veoci, and custom government systems. Our team provides certified integration support.
What international certifications does your equipment meet?
Equipment meets ISO 13485, WHO Emergency Medical Kits specifications, FEMA typing standards, and Sphere humanitarian minimums. Market-specific certifications (CE, FDA, etc.) are documented by product and available on request.
Do you provide training for field teams?
Comprehensive training programs are available — from remote orientation webinars to on-site field exercises with certified instructors. Training is included in Incident Command and National Command tiers.
How does CBRN equipment procurement work?
CBRN modules require agency verification and a dedicated National Command consultation. We work with authorized national authorities and international bodies to ensure compliant procurement and operational readiness.
