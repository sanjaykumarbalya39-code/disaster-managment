FAQ

Eco-Tourism Experience Questions

Common questions from travelers planning their first or next sustainable nature adventure with EcoVoyage.

What level of fitness do I need for eco-tourism tours?
It depends on the tour type. Explorer packages are suitable for all fitness levels with easy to moderate walks. Conservationist packages may involve half-day treks and light conservation work. Expedition programs can require good fitness for long hikes, diving, or high-altitude wildlife tracking. We clearly rate each experience and match you to the right level during your planning consultation.
How do you ensure local communities genuinely benefit?
We work exclusively with community-owned or community-managed operators. A minimum of 60% of all tour revenue stays in the local economy through guide salaries, eco-lodge employment, food sourcing, and conservation project funding. We conduct annual impact audits and share results transparently with travelers.
Are your tours suitable for solo travelers?
Absolutely. Many of our travelers join as solo adventurers — our small-group format (max 8–12 people) naturally fosters connection among like-minded conservation-minded travelers. We also offer private guide options for solo travelers who prefer a fully exclusive experience.
What happens if wildlife encounters can't be guaranteed?
Wildlife is wild — we never guarantee specific sightings. However, our expert naturalist guides know habitats intimately and maximize your chances through ethical tracking methods. Even if target species aren't seen, the ecosystem richness and overall experience we provide is always exceptional and conservation-focused.
What certifications do your naturalist guides hold?
All EcoVoyage guides are certified by regional naturalist guide associations or hold equivalents (e.g., Kenya Professional Safari Guide Association, PADI for marine tours). Many are also indigenous knowledge holders trained through our own EcoVoyage Guide Certification Program developed with WWF.
