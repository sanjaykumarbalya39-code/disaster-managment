Response Protocol

From Alert to Active — Our Four-Phase Deployment Protocol

We align field commanders, logistics coordinators, and agency liaisons — from the first incident alert to full operational deployment and post-incident debrief.

  1. 1

    Assess & Alert

    Threat and scale assessment, ICS activation, and resource mapping against the incident type, affected population, and geographic zone.

  2. 2

    Stage & Dispatch

    Pre-positioned depot activation, equipment serialization, air-freight coordination, and forward base establishment within target zone.

  3. 3

    Deploy & Operate

    Field teams on the ground, real-time command integration active, inter-agency coordination running through our live dashboard.

  4. 4

    Review & Restock

    Post-incident debrief, resource accounting, lessons-learned documentation, and depot restocking for the next activation.

FAQ

Operational Questions, Answered

Common questions from procurement offices, field commanders, and agency coordinators.

How quickly can equipment be deployed after an incident is declared?
Standard activation from our nearest depot is 24–72 hours depending on your region and agreement tier. Agencies with pre-positioned agreements under the Incident Command package achieve same-day activation in most cases.
Can your command platform integrate with our existing EOC software?
Yes. Our platform supports API integration with WebEOC, Veoci, NC4, and custom government systems. Our integration team provides certified setup and testing as part of the onboarding process.
What CBRN capabilities do you provide?
Our CBRN module includes chemical agent detection equipment, mass decontamination stations, Level A/B/C PPE stockpiles, radiological monitoring systems, and certified CBRN response protocols — available to verified national-command agencies.
Do you support multi-agency joint operations?
Yes. Our command integration layer is specifically designed for multi-agency environments, supporting ICS Section coordination, shared resource visibility, and cross-agency reporting on a single operational platform.
What training certifications do your instructors hold?
Training staff hold FEMA IS/G certifications, ICS 100–400, and UN-OCHA coordination credentials. Field exercise instructors are former incident commanders with active disaster deployment experience.
