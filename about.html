Our Story

Travel That Restores, Not Harms

Founded in 2012 by marine biologists and conservation experts, Stackly-Eco proved that tourism could be a powerful force for environmental protection. Through strategic partnerships with 200+ local conservation organizations and indigenous communities, we've generated $8.5 million for wildlife protection, habitat restoration, and community development projects. Every journey with us directly supports verified conservation outcomes.

  • Direct partnerships with local conservation organizations in 45 countries
  • Carbon-neutral operations through verified reforestation and offset programs
  • Fair-trade pricing ensuring 60% of revenue reaches conservation and communities
  • Scientific collaboration with WWF, The Nature Conservancy, and IUCN

Our Journey

From Nature Enthusiasts to a Global Eco-Tourism Platform

From our first guided rainforest walks to coordinating conservation travel across 45 countries and the world's most diverse ecosystems.

  1. 01 2012

    Founded by Conservationists

    Launched by marine biologists and wildlife conservationists with a simple belief: tourism done right can fund conservation. Our first tours in Costa Rica and the Maldives set the standard for what responsible eco-tourism should look like.

  2. 2015 02

    Africa & Asia Expansion

    Expanded to African wildlife reserves and Southeast Asian rainforests, forming direct partnerships with indigenous community guides, WWF conservation scientists, and Rainforest Alliance-certified operators.

  3. 2019 03

    Carbon Neutral Operations

    Launched our fully carbon-neutral operations model — calculating footprints for every journey, offsetting emissions through verified reforestation, and introducing our 10-trees-per-booking pledge in partnership with global reforestation NGOs.

  4. Today 04

    45 Countries, $8.5M+ for Conservation

    Operating across 45 countries with 150+ certified destinations, 200+ local conservation partner organizations, and over 50,000 travelers who have helped us raise $8.5 million for wildlife protection and community development.

Leadership Values

How We Operate in the Field

🌿

Conservation First

Every destination, guide partnership, and itinerary decision is evaluated for its environmental impact. We say no to operations that can't demonstrate genuine conservation benefit.

🤝

Community Empowerment

Local communities are partners, not backdrops. A minimum of 60% of all tour revenue is channeled directly to local guides, businesses, and conservation programs.

🌍

Transparent Impact

Every traveler receives a post-trip impact report showing exactly where their money went — trees planted, wildlife monitored, and community programs funded by their journey.
